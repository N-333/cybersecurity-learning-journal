## Overview  
This module introduced the fundamental principles of cybersecurity. It covered the cybersecurity landscape, different types of attacks, key security frameworks, and the importance of ethics in maintaining trust and compliance.

---

## Role of a Security Analyst  
- Understands and monitors security events to detect and respond to potential threats.  
- Protects organizational assets by implementing preventive and corrective controls.  
- Communicates incidents effectively and contributes to improving defense mechanisms.

---

## Cyber Attacks and Threat Actors  
- Studied various cyber attack types, including:  
  - Phishing and spear-phishing  
  - Malware and ransomware  
  - Social engineering attacks  
  - Denial-of-Service (DoS) and insider threats  
- Reviewed historical malware and early viruses to understand how modern attacks evolved.  
- Learned about different threat actors such as script kiddies, hacktivists, cybercriminals, nation-states, and insider threats, along with their motivations and techniques.

---

## Security Frameworks and Regulations  
Gained an understanding of major frameworks and regulations that guide security operations, including:  
- NIST CSF (Cybersecurity Framework): A structured approach for identifying, protecting, detecting, responding to, and recovering from threats.  
- NIST RMF (Risk Management Framework): Framework for identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks.  
- GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation): European law for protecting personal data and privacy.  
- HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act): U.S. regulation that safeguards health information.  

These frameworks emphasize compliance, risk management, and maintaining data confidentiality and integrity.

---

## Security Ethics  
- Explored the ethical principles guiding cybersecurity professionals, including confidentiality, integrity, accountability, and transparency.  
- Learned how ethical decision-making impacts cybersecurity operations, particularly when balancing user privacy with system monitoring.  
- Recognized that ethical conduct and trust are foundational to every cybersecurity role.
